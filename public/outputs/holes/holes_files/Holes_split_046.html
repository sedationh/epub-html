Holes

Louis Sachar
previous page next page

Chapter 43
第 43 章

“We weren’t always homeless,” Zero said. “I remember a yellow room.”
“W我们并不总是无家可归，”Zero 说。“我记得一个黄色的房间。”

“How old were you when you . . . ” Stanley started to ask, but couldn’t find the right words. “ . . . moved out?”
“你几岁的时候……搬出去的？”斯坦利开始问，但找不到合适的话。

“I don’t know. I must have been real little, because I don’t remember too much. I don’t remember moving out. I remember standing in a crib, with my mother singing to me. She held my wrists and made my hands clap together. She used to sing that song to me. That one you sang . . . It was different, though . . . ”
“我不知道。我一定很小，因为我记得不太多。我不记得搬出去的事。我记得我站在婴儿床里，妈妈在唱歌给我听。她握着我的手腕，让我的手拍在一起。她以前常常唱那首歌给我听。就是你唱的那首……不过那是不同的……”

Zero spoke slowly, as if searching his brain for memories and clues. “And then later I know we lived on the street, but I don’t know why we left the house. I’m pretty sure it was a house, and not an apartment. I know my room was yellow.”
零慢慢地说，好像在脑海中寻找记忆和线索。“然后我知道我们后来住在街上，但我不知道我们为什么离开了那所房子。我很确定那是一所房子，而不是一间公寓。我知道我的房间是黄色的。”

It was late afternoon. They were resting in the shadow of the Thumb. They had spent the morning picking onions and putting them in the sack. It didn’t take long, but long enough so that they had to wait another day before heading down the mountain.
下午晚些时候。他们在拇指的阴影下休息。他们花了早上的时间采洋葱并把它们放进袋子里。虽然时间不长，但足够让他们不得不再等一天才能下山。

They wanted to leave at the first hint of daylight, so they’d have plenty of time to make it to Camp Green Lake before dark. Stanley wanted to be sure he could find the right hole. Then, they would hide by it until everyone went to sleep.
他们想在第一缕曙光出现时就离开，这样他们就有足够的时间在天黑之前到达绿湖营地。斯坦利想确保自己能找到正确的洞。然后，他们会在洞旁藏起来，直到每个人都睡着。

They would dig for as long as it seemed safe, and not a second longer. And then, treasure or no treasure, they’d head up the dirt road. If it was absolutely safe, they’d try to steal some food and water from the camp kitchen.
他们会挖掘直到看起来安全为止，绝不会多挖一秒钟。然后，无论有没有宝藏，他们都会沿着土路返回。如果绝对安全，他们会试图从营地厨房偷一些食物和水。

“I’m good at sneaking in and out of places,” Zero had said.
“我擅长悄悄进出地方，”Zero 说。

“Remember,” Stanley had warned. “The door to the Wreck Room squeaks.”
“记住，”斯坦利警告道。“废墟室的门会吱吱作响。”

Now he lay on his back, trying to save his strength for the long days ahead. He wondered what happened to Zero’s parents, but he didn’t ask. Zero didn’t like answering questions. It was better to just let him talk when he felt like it.
现在他仰躺着，试图为接下来的漫长日子保存体力。他想知道零的父母发生了什么，但他没有问。零不喜欢回答问题。最好是让他在想说的时候自己说。

Stanley thought about his own parents. In her last letter, his mom was worried that they might be evicted from their apartment because of the smell of burning sneakers. They could easily become homeless as well.
斯坦利想到了自己的父母。在她的最后一封信中，他的妈妈担心他们可能会因为烧鞋子的味道而被驱逐出公寓。他们也可能很容易变得无家可归。

Again, he wondered if they’d been told that he ran away from camp. Were they told that he was dead?
他再次想知道他们是否被告知他从营地逃跑了。他们被告知他已经死了吗？

An image appeared in his head of his parents hugging each other and crying. He tried not to think about it.
他脑海中浮现出父母相拥而泣的画面。他努力不去想它。

Instead he tried to recapture the feelings he’d had the night before—the inexplicable feeling of happiness, the sense of destiny. But those feelings didn’t return.
相反，他试图重新找回前一晚的感觉——那种无法解释的幸福感和命运感。但这些感觉并没有回来。

He just felt scared.
他只是感到害怕。

The next morning they headed down the mountain. They’d dunked their caps in the water hole before putting them on their heads. Zero held the shovel, and Stanley carried the sack, which was crammed with onions and the three jars of water. They left the pieces of the broken jar on the mountain.
第二天早上，他们下山了。他们在戴上帽子之前把帽子浸入水坑中。零拿着铲子，斯坦利背着装满洋葱和三瓶水的袋子。他们把破碎的罐子碎片留在了山上。

“This is where I found the shovel,” Stanley said, pointing out a patch of weeds.
“这是我找到铲子的地方，”斯坦利说，指着一片杂草。

Zero turned and looked up toward the top of the mountain. “That’s a long way.”
零转过身，抬头望向山顶。“那真是很远。”

“You were light,” Stanley said. “You’d already thrown up everything that was inside your stomach.”
“你是光，”斯坦利说。“你已经把胃里所有的东西都吐出来了。”

He shifted the sack from one shoulder to the other. It was heavy. He stepped on a loose rock, slipped, then fell hard. The next thing he knew he was sliding down the steep side of the mountain. He dropped the sack, and onions spilled around him.
他把麻袋从一个肩膀换到另一个肩膀。它很重。他踩到一块松动的石头，滑了一下，然后重重摔倒。接下来他意识到自己正沿着陡峭的山坡滑下去。他扔掉了麻袋，洋葱洒落在他周围。

He slid into a patch of weeds and grabbed onto a thorny vine. The vine ripped out of the earth, but slowed him enough so that he was able to stop himself.
他滑进了一片杂草丛中，抓住了一根带刺的藤蔓。藤蔓从土里拔出，但减缓了他的速度，让他能够停下来。

“Are you all right?” Zero asked from above.
“你还好吗？”零从上面问。

Stanley groaned as he pulled a thorn out of the palm of his hand. “Yeah,” he said. He was all right. He was worried more about the jars of water.
斯坦利呻吟着，把一根刺拔出手掌。“是的，”他说。他没事。他更担心那些水罐。

Zero climbed down after him, retrieving the sack along the way. Stanley pulled some thorns out of his pant legs.
零在他后面爬下，途中捡起了袋子。斯坦利从裤腿上拔出了一些刺。

The jars hadn’t broken. The onions had protected them, like Styrofoam packing material. “Glad you didn’t do that when you were carrying me,” Zero said.
罐子没有破。洋葱保护了它们，就像泡沫包装材料。“我很高兴你在抱着我的时候没有这样做，”零说。

They’d lost about a third of the onions, but recovered many of them as they continued down the mountain. When they reached the bottom, the sun was just rising above the lake. They walked directly toward it.
他们大约损失了三分之一的洋葱，但在继续下山的过程中找回了许多。当他们到达山脚时，太阳刚刚升起在湖面上。他们直接朝着太阳走去。

Soon they stood on the edge of a cliff, looking down on the dry lake bed. Stanley wasn’t sure, but he thought he could see the remains of the Mary Lou off in the distance.
不久，他们站在悬崖边缘，俯视干涸的湖床。斯坦利不太确定，但他觉得在远处可以看到玛丽·露的残骸。

“You thirsty?” Stanley asked.
“你渴了吗？”斯坦利问。

“No,” said Zero. “How about you.”
“不，”零说。“你呢？”

“No,” Stanley lied. He didn’t want to be the first one to take a drink. Although they didn’t mention it, it had become a kind of challenge between him and Zero.
“不，”斯坦利撒谎道。他不想第一个喝酒。虽然他们没有提到，但这在他和零之间已经变成了一种挑战。

They climbed down into the frying pan. It was a different spot from where they had climbed up. They eased themselves down from one ledge to another, and let themselves slide in other places, being especially careful with the sack.
他们爬下了煎锅。这是一个与他们爬上时不同的地方。他们从一个岩架轻轻滑到另一个岩架，在其他地方让自己滑下去，特别小心那个袋子。

Stanley could no longer see the Mary Lou, but headed in what he thought was the right direction. As the sun rose, so did the familiar haze of heat and dirt.
斯坦利再也看不见了玛丽·露，但朝着他认为正确的方向前进。随着太阳升起，熟悉的热气和尘土也随之而来。

“You thirsty?” Zero asked.
“你渴吗？”零问。

“No,” said Stanley.
“不，”斯坦利说。

“Because you have three full jars of water,” said Zero. “I thought maybe it was getting too heavy for you. If you drink some, it will lighten your load.”
“因为你有三瓶满满的水，”零说。“我想也许对你来说太重了。如果你喝一些，负担会减轻。”

“I’m not thirsty,” said Stanley. “But if you want a drink, I’ll give you some.”
“我不渴，”斯坦利说。“但如果你想喝，我可以给你一些。”

“I’m not thirsty,” said Zero. “I was just worried about you.”
“我不渴，”零说。“我只是担心你。”

Stanley smiled. “I’m a camel,” he said.
斯坦利微笑着说：“我是骆驼。”

They walked for what seemed like a very long time, and still never came across the Mary Lou. Stanley was pretty sure they were heading in the right direction. He remembered that when they left the boat, they were headed toward the setting sun. Now they were headed toward the rising sun. He knew the sun didn’t rise and set exactly in the east and west; more southeast and southwest, but he wasn’t sure how that made a difference.
他们走了似乎很长时间，仍然没有遇到玛丽·露。斯坦利很确定他们朝着正确的方向前进。他记得他们离开船时，是朝着落日的方向走的。现在他们朝着日出的方向走。他知道太阳并不是严格地在东和西升起和落下；而是更偏南东和南西，但他不确定这有什么区别。

His throat felt as if it was coated with sandpaper. “You sure you’re not thirsty?” he asked.
他的喉咙感觉像是涂上了砂纸。“你确定你不渴吗？”他问。

“Not me,” said Zero. His voice was dry and raspy.
“不是我，”零说。他的声音干涩而沙哑。

When they did finally take a drink, they agreed to do it at the same time. Zero, who was now carrying the sack, set it down and took out two jars, giving one to Stanley. They decided to save the canteen for last, since it couldn’t accidentally break.
当他们终于喝水时，他们一致决定同时进行。现在背着麻袋的零把它放下，拿出两个罐子，给了斯坦利一个。他们决定把水壶留到最后，因为它不会意外破裂。

“You know I’m not thirsty,” Stanley said, as he unscrewed the lid. “I’m just drinking so you will.”
“你知道我不渴，”斯坦利说着，拧开瓶盖。“我只是喝水，以便你会喝。”

“I’m just drinking so you will,” said Zero.
“我只是喝酒，所以你也会喝，”零说。

They clinked the jars together and, each watching the other, poured the water into their stubborn mouths.
他们把罐子碰在一起，互相注视着，将水倒入他们顽固的嘴里。

Zero was the first to spot the Mary Lou, maybe a quarter mile away, and just a little off to the right. They headed for it.
零是第一个发现玛丽·露的人，可能在四分之一英里外，稍微偏右。他们朝那个方向前进。

It wasn’t even noon yet when they reached the boat. They sat against the shady side and rested.
他们到达船时还不到中午。他们靠在阴凉的一侧休息。

“I don’t know what happened to my mother,” Zero said. “She left and never came back.”
“我不知道我母亲发生了什么，”零说。“她离开了，永远没有回来。”

Stanley peeled an onion.
斯坦利剥了一个洋葱。

“She couldn’t always take me with her,” Zero said. “Sometimes she had to do things by herself.”
“她不能总是带我一起去，”零说。“有时候她必须自己做事情。”

Stanley had the feeling that Zero was explaining things to himself.
斯坦利感觉零是在自言自语。

“She’d tell me to wait in a certain place for her. When I was real little, I had to wait in small areas, like on a porch step or a doorway. ‘Now don’t leave here until I get back,’ she’d say.
“她会让我在某个地方等她。当我还很小的时候，我必须在小地方等，比如在门廊的台阶上或门口。‘现在不要离开这里，直到我回来，’她会说。”

“I never liked it when she left. I had a stuffed animal, a little giraffe, and I’d hug it the whole time she was gone. When I got bigger I was allowed to stay in bigger areas. Like, ‘Stay on this block.’ Or, ‘Don’t leave the park.’ But even then, I still held Jaffy.”
“我从来不喜欢她离开。我有一个毛绒玩具，一个小长颈鹿，我会在她不在的时候一直抱着它。等我长大了，我被允许待在更大的地方。比如，‘待在这个街区。’或者，‘不要离开公园。’但即便如此，我还是抱着贾菲。”

Stanley guessed that Jaffy was the name of Zero’s giraffe.
斯坦利猜测贾菲是零的长颈鹿的名字。

“And then one day she didn’t come back,” Zero said. His voice sounded suddenly hollow. “I waited for her at Laney Park.”
“然后有一天她没有回来，”零说。他的声音突然听起来空洞。“我在拉尼公园等她。”

“Laney Park,” said Stanley. “I’ve been there.”
“兰尼公园，”斯坦利说。“我去过那里。”

“You know the playscape?” asked Zero. “Yeah. I’ve played on it.”
“你知道游乐场吗？”零问。“知道。我玩过。”

“I waited there for more than a month,” said Zero. “You know that tunnel that you crawl through, between the slide and the swinging bridge? That’s where I slept.”
“我在那里等了一个多月，”零说。“你知道那个你爬过的隧道吗，在滑梯和秋千桥之间？我就是在那里睡的。”

They ate four onions apiece and drank about half a jar of water. Stanley stood up and looked around. Everything looked the same in all directions.
他们每人吃了四个洋葱，喝了大约半罐水。斯坦利站起来四处张望。四周的一切看起来都是一样的。

“When I left camp, I was heading straight toward Big Thumb,” he said. “I saw the boat off to the right. So that means we have to turn a little to the left.”
“当我离开营地时，我正朝着大拇指的方向走，”他说。“我看到船在右边。所以这意味着我们需要稍微向左转。”

Zero was lost in thought. “What? Okay,” he said.
零陷入了沉思。“什么？好吧，”他说。

They headed out. It was Stanley’s turn to carry the sack.
他们出发了。轮到斯坦利背那个袋子了。

“Some kids had a birthday party,” Zero said. “I guess it was about two weeks after my mother left. There was a picnic table next to the playscape and balloons were tied to it. The kids looked to be the same age as me. One girl said hi to me and asked me if I wanted to play. I wanted to, but I didn’t. I knew I didn’t belong at the party, even though it wasn’t their playscape. There was this one mother who kept staring at me like I was some kind of monster. Then later a boy asked me if I wanted a piece of cake, but then that same mother told me, ‘Go away!’ and she told all the kids to stay away from me, so I never got the piece of cake. I ran away so fast, I forgot Jaffy.”
“有些孩子举办了生日派对，”零说。“我想是在我母亲离开后的大约两周。游乐场旁边有一张野餐桌，上面绑着气球。那些孩子看起来和我差不多大。一个女孩跟我打了个招呼，问我想不想玩。我想玩，但我没有。我知道我不属于那个派对，尽管那不是他们的游乐场。有一个妈妈一直盯着我看，就像我是什么怪物一样。后来一个男孩问我想不想吃一块蛋糕，但那个妈妈告诉我，‘走开！’她还告诉所有孩子远离我，所以我从来没有吃到那块蛋糕。我跑得太快了，忘了贾菲。”

“Did you ever find him—it?”
“你找到他了吗——它？”

For a moment, Zero didn’t answer. Then he said, “He wasn’t real.”
有一瞬间，零没有回答。然后他说：“他不是真实的。”

Stanley thought again about his own parents, how awful it would be for them to never know if he was dead or alive. He realized that was how Zero must have felt, not knowing what happened to his own mother. He wondered why Zero never mentioned his father.
斯坦利再次想到了自己的父母，想象他们永远不知道他是死是活会有多可怕。他意识到这就是零的感受，不知道自己母亲发生了什么。他想知道为什么零从未提到过他的父亲。

“Hold on,” Zero said, stopping abruptly. “We’re going the wrong way.”
“等一下，”零突然停下来说。“我们走错方向了。”

“No, this is right,” said Stanley.
“不，这样是对的，”斯坦利说。

“You were heading toward Big Thumb when you saw the boat off to your right,” said Zero. “That means we should have turned right when we left the boat.”
“你们在朝大拇指方向前进时，看到了右边的船，”零说。“这意味着我们离开船时应该向右转。”

“You sure?”
“你确定吗？”

Zero drew a diagram in the dirt.
零在土里画了一个图。

Stanley still wasn’t sure.
斯坦利仍然不确定。

“We need to go this way,” Zero said, first drawing a line on the map and then heading that way himself.
“我们需要往这边走，”零说，先在地图上画了一条线，然后自己朝那个方向走去。

Stanley followed. It didn’t feel right to him, but Zero seemed sure.
斯坦利跟着。他觉得这不太对，但零似乎很确定。

Sometime in the middle of the afternoon, a cloud drifted across the sky and blocked out the sun. It was a welcome relief. Once again, Stanley felt that destiny was on his side.
在下午的某个时候，一朵云飘过天空，遮住了太阳。这是一个令人欢迎的缓解。斯坦利再次感到命运站在他这一边。

Zero stopped and held out his arm to stop Stanley, too.
零停下了，伸出手臂阻止斯坦利。

“Listen,” Zero whispered.
“听着，”零低声说。

Stanley didn’t hear anything.
斯坦利没有听到任何声音。

They continued walking very quietly and Stanley began to make out the faint sounds of Camp Green Lake. They were still too far away to see the camp, but he could hear a blend of indistinct voices. As they got closer he occasionally could hear Mr. Sir’s distinctive bark.
他们继续非常安静地走着，斯坦利开始听到绿湖营地微弱的声音。他们离营地仍然太远，看不见，但他能听到模糊的声音混合在一起。随着他们靠近，他偶尔能听到西尔先生独特的吼声。

They walked slowly and quietly, aware that sounds travel in both directions.
他们走得慢而安静，意识到声音是双向传播的。

They approached a cluster of holes. “Let’s wait here, until they go in,” said Zero.
他们走近一群洞。“我们在这里等，直到他们进去，”零说。

Stanley nodded. He checked to make sure there was nothing living in it, then climbed down into a hole. Zero climbed into the one next to him.
斯坦利点了点头。他检查了一下，确保里面没有生物，然后爬进了一个洞里。零爬进了他旁边的一个洞。

Despite having gone the wrong way for a while, it hadn’t taken them nearly as long as Stanley had expected. Now, they just had to wait.
尽管他们走错了路一段时间，但这并没有花费他们像斯坦利预期的那么久。现在，他们只需等待。

The sun cut through the cloud, and Stanley felt its rays beating down on him. But soon more clouds filled the sky, shading Stanley and his hole.
太阳穿透了云层，斯坦利感受到阳光照射在他身上。但很快，更多的云彩填满了天空，遮住了斯坦利和他的洞。

He waited until he was certain the last of the campers had finished for the day.
他等到确信最后一个露营者今天已经结束。

Then he waited a little longer.
然后他再等了一会儿。

As quietly as possible, he and Zero climbed up out of their holes and crept toward camp. Stanley held the sack in front of him, cradled in his arms, instead of over his shoulder, to keep the jars from clanking against each other. A wave of terror rushed over him when he saw the compound—the tents, the Wreck Room, the Warden’s cabin under the two oak trees. The fear made him dizzy. He took a breath, summoned his courage, and continued.
他和零尽可能安静地爬出洞穴，悄悄朝营地走去。斯坦利把袋子抱在怀里，而不是扛在肩上，以防罐子相互碰撞发出声响。当他看到营地——帐篷、废弃房间、两棵橡树下的监工小屋时，一阵恐惧涌上心头。恐惧让他感到头晕。他深吸一口气，鼓起勇气，继续前行。

“That’s the one,” he whispered, pointing out the hole where he had found the gold tube. It was still about fifty yards away, but Stanley was pretty sure it was the right hole. There was no need to risk going any closer.
“就是那个，”他低声说，指着他找到金管的洞。距离还有大约五十码，但斯坦利很确定那是正确的洞。没有必要冒险靠得更近。

They climbed down into adjacent holes, and waited for the camp to fall asleep.
他们爬进相邻的洞里，等待营地入睡。
