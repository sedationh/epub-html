15 Use Tools and Protocols to Shape How Work Is Done
15 使用工具和协议来塑造工作完成方式

Words alone aren’t enough.
仅靠 Words 是不够的。

That’s something I learned from watching people struggle to get themselves to do things that are in their best interests. After I shared these principles with the people at Bridgewater and refined them, nearly everyone saw the connection between the principles and our excellent results and wanted to operate in accordance with them. But there’s a big difference between wanting to do something and actually being able to do it. Assuming people will do what they intellectually want to do is like assuming that people will lose weight simply because they understand why it’s beneficial for them to do it. It won’t happen until the proper habits are developed. In organizations, that happens with the help of tools and protocols.
这是我从观察人们努力让自己做符合他们最大利益的事情中学到的东西。在我与 Bridgewater 的员工分享并完善这些原则后，几乎每个人都看到了这些原则与我们的卓越结果之间的联系，并希望按照这些原则进行操作。但是，想要做某事和真正能够做某事之间有很大的区别。假设人们会做他们在理智上想做的事情，就像假设人们会减肥仅仅是因为他们明白为什么这样做对他们有益。在养成正确的习惯之前，它不会发生。在组织中，这是在工具和协议的帮助下实现的。

Take a minute to think about how this applies to your reading of this book, or reading books in general. How often have you read a book describing some behavioral change you’ve wanted to make but then failed to? How much behavioral change do you think will result from this book if you don’t have tools and protocols to help you? My guess is hardly any. Just as you can’t learn many things by reading a book (how to ride a bike, speak a language, etc.), it’s nearly impossible to change a behavior without practicing it. That is why I plan to make the tools that I describe in the Appendix publicly available.
花点时间想一想这如何适用于您阅读这本书或一般的书籍。你有多少次读过一本书，描述你曾想做出一些行为改变，但后来没有做到？如果您没有工具和协议来帮助您，您认为这本书会带来多少行为变化？我的猜测几乎没有。就像你不能通过阅读一本书学到很多东西（如何骑自行车、说一门语言等）一样，如果不练习，几乎不可能改变行为。这就是为什么我计划公开我在附录中描述的工具。

15.1 Having systemized principles embedded in tools is especially valuable for an idea meritocracy.
15.1 将系统化的原则嵌入到工具中对于思想精英管理特别有价值。

That is because an idea meritocracy needs to operate in accordance with agreed-upon principles and to be evidence-based and fair instead of following the more autocratic and arbitrary decisions of the CEO and his or her lieutenants. Rather than be above the principles, the people responsible for running the organization must be evaluated, chosen, and—if needed—replaced in an evidence-based way according to rules, just like everyone else in the organization. Their strengths and weaknesses, like everyone’s, must be taken into consideration. Collecting objective data about all people is essential for this. And you need good tools to convert data into decisions in agreed-upon ways. Moreover, the tools allow the people and the system to work together in a symbiotic way to improve each other.
这是因为，精英政治需要按照商定的原则运作，并以证据为基础和公平，而不是遵循首席执行官及其副手更加专制和武断的决定。负责运营组织的人必须像组织中的其他人一样，按照规则以循证的方式进行评估、选择和更换，而不是凌驾于原则之上。他们的长处和短处，就像每个人的长处和短处一样，必须被考虑在内。为此，收集有关所有人的客观数据至关重要。您需要好的工具，以商定的方式将数据转化为决策。此外，这些工具允许人们和系统以共生的方式一起工作，以相互改进。

a. To produce real behavioral change, understand that there must be internalized or habituated learning. Thankfully, technology has made internalized learning much easier today than it was when books were the primary way of conveying knowledge. Don’t get me wrong, the book was a powerful invention. Johannes Gutenberg’s printing press allowed easy dissemination of knowledge that helped people build on each other’s learnings. But experiential learning is so much more powerful. Now that technology makes it so easy to create experiential/virtual learning, I believe that we are on the brink of another step-change improvement in the quality of learning that will be as great as or even greater than Gutenberg’s.
a. 要产生真正的行为改变，要明白必须有内化或习惯化的学习。值得庆幸的是，与书籍作为传播知识的主要方式时相比，今天的技术使内化学习变得更加容易。不要误会我的意思，这本书是一项强大的发明。约翰内斯·古腾堡 （Johannes Gutenberg） 的印刷机使知识得以轻松传播，帮助人们相互借鉴。但体验式学习要强大得多。现在，技术使创建体验式/虚拟学习变得如此容易，我相信我们正处于学习质量的另一次阶跃式改进即将到来，这将与古腾堡一样好，甚至更大。

We have been trying to create internalized learning at Bridge-water for a long time, so how we do it has evolved a lot. Since we tape virtually all our meetings, we have been able to create virtual learning case studies that allow everyone to participate without actually being in the room. People see the meeting transpire as though they were in it, and then the case study pauses and asks them for their own thinking on the matter at hand. In some cases, they input their reactions in real time as they watch. Their thinking is recorded and compared with others’ using expert systems that help us all understand more about how we think. With this information, we can better tailor their learning and their job assignments to their thinking styles.
长期以来，我们一直在努力在 Bridge-water 创造内化学习，因此我们的工作方式已经发生了很大变化。由于我们几乎录制了所有会议，因此我们能够创建虚拟学习案例研究，让每个人都可以参与，而无需实际进入会议室。人们看到会议的发生，就好像他们身临其境一样，然后案例研究暂停并询问他们对手头问题的看法。在某些情况下，他们会在观看时实时输入自己的反应。他们的思维被记录下来，并使用专家系统与他人进行比较，帮助我们更多地了解我们的思维方式。有了这些信息，我们可以更好地根据他们的思维方式定制他们的学习和工作分配。

That is just one example of a number of tools and protocols we have developed to help our people learn and operate by our principles.
这只是我们开发的众多工具和协议的一个例子，这些工具和协议旨在帮助我们的员工按照我们的原则学习和操作。

b. Use tools to collect data and process it into conclusions and actions. Imagine that virtually everything important going on in your company can be captured as data, and that you can build algorithms to instruct the computer, as you would instruct a person, to analyze that data and use it in the way you agreed it should be used. In that way, you and the computer on your behalf could look at each person and all the people together and provide tailored guidance, just like your GPS provides you guidance by knowing all the traffic patterns and routes. You don’t have to make it mandatory to follow that guidance, though you can. Generally speaking, the system operates like a coach. And the coach can learn about its team: Data is collected about what people do so that if they make more insightful moves or less insightful moves, learning will occur and be used to create improvements. Because the thinking behind the algorithms is available to everyone, anyone can assess the quality of the logic and its fairness, and have a hand in shaping it.
b. 使用工具收集数据并将其处理成结论和行动。想象一下，您公司中发生的几乎所有重要事情都可以作为数据捕获，并且您可以构建算法来指示计算机，就像您指示一个人一样，分析这些数据并按照您同意的方式使用它。这样，您和代表您的计算机可以一起查看每个人和所有人并提供量身定制的指导，就像您的 GPS 通过了解所有交通模式和路线为您提供指导一样。您不必强制要求遵循该指南，但您可以这样做。一般来说，该系统的运作就像一辆客车。教练可以了解其团队：收集有关人们所做的事情的数据，这样，如果他们做出更有洞察力的举动或更有洞察力的举动，就会发生学习并用于创造改进。因为每个人都可以了解算法背后的思想，所以任何人都可以评估逻辑的质量及其公平性，并参与塑造它。

c. Foster an environment of confidence and fairness by having clearly-stated principles that are implemented in tools and protocols so that the conclusions reached can be assessed by tracking the logic and data behind them. In all organizations, it’s always the case that some of the people judged to be ineffective will argue that those judgments are wrong. When that happens, a data- and rules-based system with clearly laid-out criteria allows less room for such arguments and greater belief that the system is fair. Though the system won’t be perfect, it is much less arbitrary—and can much more easily be examined for bias—than the much less specified and much less open decision making of individuals with authority. My ideal is to have a process in which everyone contributes criteria for good decision making and those criteria are assessed and selected by appropriately assigned (believable) people. If people maintain the right balance of open-mindedness and assertiveness so they understand where they are and aren’t believable to make decisions, having these open discussions on the criteria for assessing and managing people can be very powerful in building and reinforcing the idea meritocracy.
c. 通过在工具和协议中实施明确陈述的原则来营造一个自信和公平的环境，以便通过跟踪其背后的逻辑和数据来评估得出的结论。在所有组织中，总是一些被判断为无效的人会争辩说这些判断是错误的。当这种情况发生时，一个基于数据和规则的系统，具有明确制定的标准，就会减少这种争论的空间，并更相信这个系统是公平的。虽然这个系统不会完美无缺，但与权威人士的决策相比，它的武断性要小得多，也更容易被检查是否存在偏见。我的理想是有一个过程，在这个过程中，每个人都为做出良好决策提供标准，并且这些标准由适当分配（可信的）人员进行评估和选择。如果人们在开放的思想和自信之间保持适当的平衡，这样他们就了解自己在哪里，并且不可信地做出决策，那么就评估和管理人员的标准进行这些公开讨论，可以非常有效地建立和加强精英管理理念。

We have early-stage tools that achieve these things and we are striving to refine them so that our people management system operates as effectively as our investment management system.
我们拥有实现这些目标的早期工具，并且正在努力改进它们，以便我们的人员管理系统与我们的投资管理系统一样有效地运行。

Even with its imperfections, our evidence-based approach to learning about people, guiding them, and sorting them is much fairer and more effective than the arbitrary and subjective management systems that most organizations still rely on. I believe that the forces of evolution will push most organizations toward systems that combine human and computer intelligence to program principles into algorithms that substantially improve decision making.
即使存在缺陷，我们基于证据的方法来了解人们、指导他们和对他们进行分类，也比大多数组织仍然依赖的武断和主观的管理系统更公平、更有效。我相信，进化的力量将推动大多数组织转向将人类和计算机智能相结合的系统，将原理编程成算法，从而大大改善决策。

In the Appendix, I’ve provided detailed descriptions of a number of the tools and protocols that support this idea-meritocratic approach and reinforce the behaviors that people need to operate consistently with it. They are designed to help us achieve our goals of 1) learning what people are like, 2) sharing what people are like, 3) providing personalized training and development, 4) offering guidance and oversight in specific situations, and 5) helping managers sort people into the right roles or out of the company based on what they are like and what is required.
在附录中，我详细描述了许多工具和协议，这些工具和协议支持这种理念-精英方法，并加强了人们需要始终如一地使用它的行为。它们旨在帮助我们实现以下目标：1） 了解人们是什么样的，2） 分享人们是什么样的，3） 提供个性化的培训和发展，4） 在特定情况下提供指导和监督，以及 5） 帮助经理根据他们的特点和要求将人们分配到合适的角色或离开公司。

You don’t need to use these same tools and protocols for your own idea meritocracy, but you should have ways of producing the internalized learning that it will require. While ours have evolved a lot, yours don’t have to be as fancy or automated. For example, providing a form or a template to help guide people through the steps required for them to manage their work or carry out a process will yield better results than expecting them to just remember—or figure it out—on their own.
你不需要为你自己的想法精英管理使用这些相同的工具和协议，但你应该有办法产生它所需的内化学习。虽然我们的已经发展了很多，但您的不必那么花哨或自动化。例如，提供表单或模板来帮助指导人们完成管理工作或执行流程所需的步骤，这比期望他们自己记住或弄清楚要产生更好的结果。

How you decide to use tools and protocols is up to you. The main point I want to make here is that they’re important.
您决定如何使用工具和协议取决于您。我想在这里强调的重点是它们很重要。
