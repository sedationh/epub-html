12 Diagnose Problems to Get at Their Root Causes
12 诊断问题以找出根本原因

When you encounter problems, your objective is to specifically identify the root causes of those problems—the specific people or designs that caused them—and to see if these people or designs have a pattern of causing problems.
当您遇到问题时，您的目标是专门确定这些问题的根本原因 — 导致这些问题的特定人员或设计 — 并查看这些人员或设计是否具有导致问题的模式。

What are the most common reasons for failing to diagnose well?
诊断不佳的最常见原因是什么？

The most common mistake I see people make is dealing with their problems as one-offs rather than using them to diagnose how their machine is working so that they can improve it. They move on to fix problems without getting at their root causes, which is a recipe for continued failure. A thorough and accurate diagnosis, while more time-consuming, will pay huge dividends in the future.
我看到人们最常犯的错误是将他们的问题作为一次性问题来处理，而不是使用它们来诊断他们的机器是如何工作的，以便他们能够改进它。他们继续解决问题，但没有找到根本原因，这是持续失败的秘诀。彻底而准确的诊断虽然更耗时，但将在未来获得巨大的回报。

The second most common mistake people make is to depersonalize the diagnosis. Not connecting problems to the people who failed and not examining what it is about them that caused the failure will not lead to improvements of the individuals or the machines.
人们常犯的第二个错误是去个性化诊断。不将问题与失败的人联系起来，不检查导致失败的原因，不会导致个人或机器的改进。

The third biggest reason for failure is to not connect what one is learning in one diagnosis to what was learned in prior ones. It is important to determine whether the root cause of a particular problem (“Harry was careless”) is part of a larger pattern (“Harry is often careless”) or not (“It’s unlike Harry to be careless”).
失败的第三大原因是没有将一个人在一次诊断中学到的东西与在以前的诊断中学到的东西联系起来。重要的是要确定特定问题的根本原因（“Harry 很粗心”）是更大模式的一部分（“Harry 经常粗心大意”）还是不是（“粗心大意与 Harry 不同”）。

In the case of our client service analytics team, I knew that unless we got to the root cause of the problems, standards would continue to decline. Bridgewater’s other leaders agreed. So I led a series of diagnostic sessions with the team, getting everyone at every level into the room to probe and find out what had gone wrong. I started with my mental map of how things should’ve gone—based on the machine I’d built—and asked the new managers to describe what had actually happened. Bad outcomes don’t just happen; they occur because specific people make, or fail to make, specific decisions. A good diagnosis always gets to the level of determining what it is about those people that led to the bad outcomes. This can be uncomfortable but if someone isn’t suited for a job, they need to be moved out of it so that the same mistakes won’t keep occurring. Of course, nobody is perfect; everyone makes mistakes. So it is important to look at people’s track records and their specific strengths and weaknesses in doing a diagnosis.
就我们的客户服务分析团队而言，我知道除非我们找到问题的根本原因，否则标准将继续下降。布里奇沃特的其他领导人也同意这一点。因此，我与团队进行了一系列诊断会议，让各个级别的每个人都进入房间，探究出了什么问题。我从我的脑海地图开始——根据我建造的机器——并要求新的经理描述实际发生的事情。坏的结果不会随便发生;它们之所以发生，是因为特定的人做出或没有做出特定的决定。一个好的诊断总是达到确定那些人是什么导致了不良结果的层面。这可能会让人不舒服，但如果有人不适合某份工作，他们需要被搬离这份工作，这样同样的错误就不会继续发生。当然，没有人是完美的;每个人都会犯错。因此，重要的是要查看人们的业绩记录以及他们在进行诊断时的具体优势和劣势。

Coming out of these sessions, a few things were clear: Several of the new line managers who the top managers had brought in to run client service analytics didn’t have the right skills, synthesis abilities, or levels of caring to oversee the quality-control process; and the top managers were far too distant from the area and not probing adequately to make sure that everything was going well. This was the “what is”—the reality we faced that produced our problems. It wasn’t a pretty picture, but it was exactly what we needed to know in order to move to the next step of designing the changes we had to make.
从这些会议中得出的结论是：高层经理带来的几位新直线经理来运行客户服务分析，他们不具备监督质量控制流程的适当技能、综合能力或关怀水平;而且高层管理人员离该地区太远了，没有进行充分的调查以确保一切顺利。这就是 “现状” — 我们面临的现实产生了我们的问题。这不是一幅漂亮的图景，但这正是我们需要了解的内容，以便进入下一步设计我们必须做出的更改。

The following principles flesh out how to diagnose well, beginning with a basic overview.
以下原则充实了如何进行良好诊断，从基本概述开始。

12.1 To diagnose well, ask the following questions:
12.1 为了诊断得好，请问以下问题：

1. Is the outcome good or bad?
1. 结果是好是坏？

2. Who is responsible for the outcome?
2. 谁对结果负责？

3. If the outcome is bad, is the Responsible Party incapable and/or is the design bad?
3. 如果结果不好，责任方是否有能力和/或设计不好？

If you keep those big questions in mind and anchor back to them, you should do well. What follows is a guide for getting the answers to these big-picture questions, mostly using a series of simple either/or questions to help you get to the synthesis you are looking for at each step. You should think of these as the answers you need before moving to the next step, leading all the way to the final diagnosis.
如果你牢记这些大问题并回到它们，你应该做得很好。以下是获得这些大局问题答案的指南，主要使用一系列简单的非此即彼的问题来帮助您在每个步骤中找到您正在寻找的综合。在进行下一步之前，您应该将这些视为您需要的答案，一直引导到最终诊断。

You can, but don’t need to, follow these questions or this format exactly. Depending on your circumstances, you may be able to move through these questions quickly or you may need to ask some different, more granular questions.
您可以（但不需要）完全按照这些问题或此格式进行操作。根据您的情况，您可能能够快速回答这些问题，或者您可能需要提出一些不同的、更细致的问题。

Is the outcome good or bad? And who is responsible for the outcome? If you can’t quickly get in sync that the outcome was bad and who specifically was responsible, you’re probably already headed for the weeds (in other words, into a discussion of tiny, irrelevant details).
结果是好是坏？谁对结果负责？如果你不能快速同步结果很糟糕以及具体责任人是谁，那么你可能已经走向了杂草（换句话说，进入了对微小的、不相关的细节的讨论）。

If the outcome is bad, is the RP incapable and/or is the design bad? The goal is to come to this synthesis, though to get there you may need to examine how the machine worked in this instance and build the synthesis from there.
如果结果很糟糕，RP 是否无能和/或设计是否糟糕？目标是实现此合成，但要达到此目标，您可能需要检查此实例中机器的工作原理，并从那里构建合成。

How should the machine have worked? You may have a mental map of who should have done what, or you may need to fill it in using other people’s mental maps. In any case, you need to learn who was responsible for doing what and what the principles say about how things should’ve gone. Keep it simple! At this stage, a common pitfall is to delve into a granular examination of procedural details rather than stay at the level of the machine (the level of who was responsible for doing what). You should be able to crystallize your mental map in just a few statements, each connected to a specific person. If you are delving into details here, you are probably off track. Once you’ve established the mental map the key question is:
机器应该如何工作？你可能有一个关于谁应该做什么的心理地图，或者你可能需要使用其他人的心理地图来填写它。无论如何，你需要了解谁负责做什么，以及原则对事情应该如何发展是怎么说的。保持简单！在这个阶段，一个常见的陷阱是深入研究程序细节的精细检查，而不是停留在机器的层面（谁负责做什么的层面）。你应该能够用几句话来具体化你的心理地图，每句话都与一个特定的人有关。如果您在这里深入研究细节，您可能偏离了轨道。一旦你建立了心智导图，关键问题是：

Did the machine work as it should have? Yes or no.
机器是否正常工作？是或否。

If not, what didn’t go as it should have? What broke? This is called the proximate cause and this step should be easy to get to if you laid out the mental map clearly. You can do this via yes/no questions as well because it should just require referring back to the key components of your mental map and pinpointing which the RP or RPs didn’t do well.
如果不是，什么没有按照应有的方式进行？什么坏了？这称为 proximate cause，如果您清楚地布置了心理地图，这一步应该很容易到达。您也可以通过是/否问题来做到这一点，因为它只需要回顾您的心理地图的关键组成部分并指出哪些 RP 或 RP 做得不好。

Say your mental map of how the machine should have worked has two steps: that Harry should have either 1) done his assignment on time or 2) escalated that he couldn’t. All you have to do is pinpoint the two steps. 1) Did he do it on time? Yes or no. And if not, 2) did he escalate? Yes or no.
假设你对机器应该如何工作的心理地图有两个步骤：Harry 应该 1） 按时完成他的作业，或者 2） 升级到他无法完成的任务。您所要做的就是确定这两个步骤。1） 他按时完成吗？是或否。如果没有，2） 他升级了吗？是或否。

It should be this simple. But this is when the conversation often gets dragged into gobbledygook, where someone goes into a detailed explanation of “what they did.” Remember: It’s your job to guide the conversation toward an accurate and clear synthesis.
应该这么简单。但此时，谈话经常被拖入 gobbledygook，有人详细解释 “他们做了什么”。记住：你的工作是引导对话朝着准确和清晰的综合方向发展。

You also have to synthesize whether the problem was meaningful—that is, whether a capable person would have made the same mistake given the circumstances, or whether it’s symptomatic of something worth digging into. Don’t focus too much on rare events or the trivial problems—nothing and no one is perfect—but be sure you are not overlooking a clue to a systemic machine problem. It’s your job to make that determination.
你还必须综合这个问题是否有意义——也就是说，在当时的情况下，一个有能力的人是否会犯同样的错误，或者它是否是值得深入研究的征兆。不要过多关注罕见事件或琐碎的问题 — 没有什么是完美的，也没有人是完美的 — 但请确保您没有忽视系统性机器问题的线索。做出这个决定是你的工作。

Why didn’t things go as they should have? This is where you have synthesized the root cause in order to determine whether the RP is capable or not—or whether the issue is with the design. In order to anchor back to a synthesis rather than get lost in the details you might:
为什么事情没有按照他们应该的方式进行呢？这是您综合根本原因的地方，以确定 RP 是否有效，或者问题是否与设计有关。为了锚定回合成而不是迷失在细节中，您可以：

• Try to tie the failure to the 5-Step Process. Which step was not done well? Everything ultimately fits into those five steps. But you may need to get more specific, so:
• 尝试将失败与 5 步过程联系起来。哪个步骤做得不好？一切都最终适合这五个步骤。但你可能需要更具体一些，所以：

• Try to crystallize the failure as a specific key attribute or set of attributes. Ask yes/no questions: Did the RP not manage well? Not perceive problems well? Not execute well?
• 尝试将失败具体化为特定的关键属性或一组属性。提出是/否问题：RP 管理得不好吗？不能很好地感知问题？执行得不好？

• Importantly, ask yourself this question: If X attribute is done well next time, will the bad outcome still occur? This is a good way of making sure you are logically connecting the outcome back to the case. Think of it this way: If your mechanic replaced that part in your car, would that fix it?
• 重要的是，问自己这个问题：如果下次 X 属性做得很好，坏的结果还会发生吗？这是确保将结果逻辑地联系回案例的好方法。可以这样想：如果你的机械师更换了你车上的那个部件，它会修复它吗？

• If the root cause is a faulty design, don’t stop there. Ask who was responsible for the faulty design and whether they are capable of designing well.
• 如果根本原因是设计错误，请不要止步于此。询问谁对错误的设计负责，以及他们是否有能力设计得很好。

Is the root cause a pattern? (Yes or no.) Any problem can be a one-off imperfection—or it could be a symptom of a root cause that will show up repeatedly. You need to determine which it is. In other words, if Harry failed to do the assignment due to reliability:
根本原因是一种模式吗？（是或否。任何问题都可能是一次性的缺陷，也可能是反复出现的根本原因的症状。您需要确定它是哪个。换句话说，如果 Harry 因为可靠性而没有完成任务：

• Does Harry have a reliability problem in general?
• Harry 一般有可靠性问题吗？

• If so, is reliability required for the role?
• 如果是这样，该角色是否需要可靠性？

• Is Harry’s failure due to training or abilities?
• 哈利的失败是由于训练还是能力？

How should the people/machines evolve as a result? Confirm that the short-term resolution of the issue has been addressed, as needed. Determine the steps to be taken for long-term solutions and who is responsible for those steps. Specifically:
因此，人/机器应该如何进化？确认已根据需要解决了问题的短期解决方案。确定长期解决方案要采取的步骤以及负责这些步骤的人员。具体说来：

• Are there responsibilities that need to be assigned or clarified?
• 是否有需要分配或澄清的责任？

• Are there machine designs that need to be reworked?
• 是否有需要返工的机器设计？

• Are there people whose fit for their roles needs to be reevaluated?
• 是否有人适合他们的角色需要重新评估？

For example, if you’ve determined that 1) it’s a pattern, 2) the RP is missing an attribute that’s required for the role, and 3) the attribute is missing due to the RP’s ability (not their training)—then you’ve likely been able to determine the answer to your most important question: the person is not capable and needs to be sorted from the role.
例如，如果您确定 1） 这是一种模式，2） RP 缺少角色所需的属性，以及 3） 由于 RP 的能力（而不是他们的培训）而缺少该属性，那么您可能已经能够确定您最重要问题的答案：该人没有能力，需要从角色中排序。

The following principles further flesh out how to diagnose well.
以下原则进一步充实了如何进行良好诊断。

a. Ask yourself: “Who should do what differently?” I often hear people complaining about a particular outcome without attempting to understand the machine that caused it. In many cases, these complaints come from people who are seeing the cons of some decision but not the pros and don’t know how the Responsible Party weighed them to come to a decision. Since all outcomes ultimately come from people and designs, asking yourself “Who should do what differently?” will point you in the direction of the kind of understanding that you need to actually change outcomes in the future (versus just chirping about them).
a. 问问自己：“谁应该做什么不同的事情？我经常听到人们抱怨某个特定的结果，而没有试图理解导致它的机器。在许多情况下，这些投诉来自那些看到某些决定的弊端而不是优点的人，他们不知道责任方如何权衡他们以做出决定。由于所有结果最终都来自人和设计，因此问自己“谁应该以不同的方式做什么”将为您指明您需要在未来真正改变结果的理解方向（而不是仅仅唠叨它们）。

b. Identify at which step in the 5-Step Process the failure occurred. If a person is chronically failing, it is due to a lack of training or a lack of ability. Which is it? At which of the five steps did the person fail? Different steps require different abilities and if you can identify which abilities are lacking, you’ll go a long way toward diagnosing the problem.
b. 确定失败发生在 5 步流程的哪个步骤。如果一个人长期失败，那是由于缺乏训练或缺乏能力。它是什么？这个人在五个步骤中的哪一个步骤失败了？不同的步骤需要不同的能力，如果你能确定哪些能力不足，你将对诊断问题大有帮助。

c. Identify the principles that were violated. Identify which principles apply to the case at hand, review them, and see if they would have helped. Think for yourself which principles are best for handling similar cases. This will help solve not only this problem but other problems like it.
c. 确定违反的原则。确定哪些原则适用于手头的案例，审查它们，看看它们是否会有所帮助。自己想想哪些原则最适合处理类似情况。这不仅有助于解决这个问题，还有助于解决其他类似问题。

d. Avoid Monday morning quarterbacking. Evaluate the merits of a past decision based not on what you know now but only on what you could have reasonably known at the time the decision was made. Every decision has pros and cons; you can’t evaluate choices in retrospect without the appropriate context. Do this by asking yourself, “What should a quality person have known and done in that situation?” Also, have a deep understanding of the person who made the decision (how they think, the type of person they are, whether they learned from the situation, and so on).
d. 避免周一早上的四分卫。评估过去决定的优点，不要根据你现在知道的情况，而要根据你在做出决定时可以合理地知道的情况。每个决定都有利有弊;如果没有适当的背景，你就无法在回顾中评估选择。通过问自己来做到这一点，“在那种情况下，一个有素质的人应该知道什么并做什么？此外，对做出决定的人有深入的了解（他们如何思考，他们是什么样的人，他们是否从情况中吸取了教训，等等）。

e. Don’t confuse the quality of someone’s circumstances with the quality of their approach to dealing with the circumstances. One can be good and the other can be bad, and it’s easy to confuse which is which. Such confusion is especially common in organizations that are doing new things and evolving fast but haven’t yet gotten the kinks out.
e. 不要将某人的情况质量与他们处理情况的方法质量混淆。一个可能是好的，另一个可能是坏的，很容易混淆哪个是哪个。这种混淆在那些正在做新事物和快速发展但尚未解决问题的组织中尤为常见。

I have always described Bridgewater as being “terrible and terrific at the same time.” For nearly forty years, we have consistently produced extraordinary results while struggling with lots of problems. It is easy to look at messy circumstances, think things must be terrible, and get frustrated. But the real challenge is to look at the long-term successes these messy circumstances have produced and understand how essential they are to the evolutionary process of innovation.
我一直将布里奇沃特描述为“既可怕又了不起”。近四十年来，我们在与许多问题作斗争的同时，始终如一地取得非凡的成果。人们很容易看到混乱的环境，认为事情一定很糟糕，然后感到沮丧。但真正的挑战是审视这些混乱的环境所产生的长期成功，并了解它们对创新的进化过程有多么重要。

f. Identifying the fact that someone else doesn’t know what to do doesn’t mean that you know what to do. It’s one thing to point out a problem; it’s another to have an accurate diagnosis and a quality solution. As described earlier, the litmus test for a good problem solver is 1) they are able to logically describe how to handle the problem and 2) they have successfully solved similar problems in the past.
f. 确定其他人不知道该怎么做的事实并不意味着您知道该怎么做。指出问题是一回事;拥有准确的诊断和高质量的解决方案是另一回事。如前所述，一个好的问题解决者的试金石是 1） 他们能够逻辑地描述如何处理问题，以及 2） 他们过去曾成功解决过类似的问题。

g. Remember that a root cause is not an action but a reason. Root causes are described in adjectives, not verbs, so keep asking “why” to get at them. Since most things are done or not done because someone decided to do them or not do them in a certain way, most root causes can be traced to specific people who have specific patterns of behavior. Of course, a normally reliable person can make the occasional error and if that’s the case, then it can be forgiven, but when a problem is attributable to a person, you have to ask why they made the mistake—and you have to be as accurate in diagnosing a fault in a person as you would be if he or she were a piece of equipment.
g. 请记住，根本原因不是操作，而是原因。根本原因是用形容词而不是动词来描述的，所以要不断问 “为什么” 来了解它们。由于大多数事情的完成或不完成都是因为有人决定以某种方式做或不做，因此大多数根本原因都可以追溯到具有特定行为模式的特定人。当然，一个通常可靠的人偶尔会犯错误，如果是这样的话，那么可以原谅，但是当问题归咎于一个人时，你必须问他们为什么犯这个错误——而且你必须像诊断他或她是一件设备一样准确地诊断一个人的过错。

A root cause discovery process might proceed like this:
根本原因发现过程可能会按如下方式进行：

The problem was due to bad programming.
问题是由于糟糕的编程造成的。

Why was there bad programming?
为什么会有糟糕的编程？

Because Harry programmed it badly.
因为哈利编程得很糟糕。

Why did Harry program it badly?
为什么 Harry 把它编程得很糟糕？

Because he wasn’t well trained and because he was in a rush.
因为他没有受过良好的训练，而且他很匆忙。

Why wasn’t he well trained? Did his manager know that he wasn’t well trained and let him do the job anyway, or did he not know?
为什么他没有受过良好的训练？他的经理是知道他没有受过良好的培训，还是让他做这份工作，还是他不知道？

Consider how personal the questioning is. It doesn’t stop at “Because Harry programmed it badly.” You must go deeper in order to understand what about the people and/or the design led to the failure. This is difficult for both the diagnoser and the RPs, and it often results in people bringing up all kinds of irrelevant details. Be on your guard because people will often look to cover themselves by diving into the weeds.
考虑一下这种质疑是多么个人化。它并不止于“因为 Harry 编程得很糟糕”。您必须更深入地了解导致失败的人员和/或设计的原因。这对诊断者和 RP 来说都很困难，而且经常导致人们提出各种不相关的细节。要保持警惕，因为人们通常会通过潜入杂草来掩盖自己。

h. To distinguish between a capacity issue and a capability issue, imagine how the person would perform at that particular function if they had ample capacity. Think back on how the person performed in similar functions when they had ample capacity. If the same kinds of problems came up, then the problem is very likely one of capabilities.
h. 为了区分能力问题和能力问题，想象一下如果这个人有足够的能力，他们将如何执行该特定功能。回想一下，当这个人有足够的能力时，他们是如何执行类似职能的。如果出现相同类型的问题，那么问题很可能是功能问题之一。

i. Keep in mind that managers usually fail or fall short of their goals for one (or more) of five reasons.
我。请记住，管理者通常由于以下五个原因中的一个（或多个）而未能实现目标。

1. They are too distant.
1. 他们太遥远了。

2. They have problems perceiving bad quality.
2. 他们在感知质量差方面存在问题。

3. They have lost sight of how bad things have become because they have gotten used to it.
3. 他们忘记了事情已经变得多么糟糕，因为他们已经习惯了。

4. They have such high pride in their work (or such large egos) that they can’t bear to admit they are unable to solve their own problems.
4. 他们对自己的工作有如此高的自豪感（或如此大的自我），以至于他们不忍心承认他们无法解决自己的问题。

5. They fear adverse consequences from admitting failure.
5. 他们害怕承认失败的不良后果。

12.2 Maintain an emerging synthesis by diagnosing continuously.
12.2 通过持续诊断来维持新出现的综合。

If you don’t look into significant bad outcomes as they occur, you won’t be able to understand what things they are symptomatic of or how they are changing through time—i.e., are they getting better or worse?
如果你不研究发生的重大不良结果，你就无法理解它们是什么症状，或者它们是如何随着时间的推移而变化的——即，它们是变得更好还是更糟？

12.3 Keep in mind that diagnoses should produce outcomes.
12.3 请记住，诊断应该产生结果。

If they don’t, there’s no purpose to them. At a minimum, a diagnosis should take the form of theories about root causes and clarity about what information needs to be gathered to find out more. At best, it should lead directly to a plan or design to fix the problem or problems.
如果他们不这样做，他们就没有意义。至少，诊断应采用有关根本原因的理论形式，并明确需要收集哪些信息以了解更多信息。充其量，它应该直接导致解决问题的计划或设计。

a. Remember that if you have the same people doing the same things, you should expect the same results. Einstein defined insanity as doing the same thing over and over and expecting different results. Don’t fall into this trap because you’ll have a hard time getting out of it.
a. 请记住，如果让相同的人执行相同的操作，则应该会获得相同的结果。爱因斯坦将精神错乱定义为一遍又一遍地做同样的事情并期待不同的结果。不要落入这个陷阱，因为您将很难摆脱它。

12.4 Use the following “drill-down” technique to gain an 80/20 understanding of a department or sub-department that is having problems.
12,4使用以下 “向下钻取” 技术来获得对有问题的部门或子部门的 80/20 理解。

A drill-down is a process that allows you to gain an understanding of the root causes of the biggest problems in a department or area so you can design a plan to make the area excellent. Drill-downs are not diagnoses, but a form of broad and deep probing. They’re not intended to uncover the causes of every problem: only the 20 or so percent of causes that produce 80 percent of the suboptimal effects. A drill-down takes place in two steps and is then followed by design and execution steps. If done well, the two drill-down steps can be done in about four hours. It is very important that the steps be done separately and independently, so as not to go in too many directions at once. Let me take you through the drill-down process, offering guidance and examples for each step.
向下钻取是一个过程，可让您了解某个部门或领域中最大问题的根本原因，以便您可以设计一个计划来使该领域变得出色。深入分析不是诊断，而是一种广泛而深入的探测形式。它们并不是为了揭示每个问题的原因：只有 20% 左右的原因会产生 80% 的次优效果。向下钻取分两个步骤进行，然后是设计和执行步骤。如果做得好，可以在大约 4 小时内完成这两个向下钻取步骤。这些步骤单独独立完成非常重要，以免一次进入太多方向。让我带您完成向下钻取过程，为每个步骤提供指导和示例。

Step 1: List the Problems. Quickly inventory all the core problems. Be very specific, as this is the only way to effectively find solutions. Don’t generalize or use the plural “we” or “they.” Name the names of the people experiencing the problems.
第 1 步：列出问题。快速盘点所有核心问题。要非常具体，因为这是有效找到解决方案的唯一方法。不要概括或使用复数形式 “we” 或 “they”。说出遇到问题的人员的姓名。

• Have all the relevant people from the area under scrutiny participate in the drill-down; you will benefit from their insights and it will drive their ownership of the solution.
• 让受审查区域的所有相关人员参与深入分析;您将从他们的见解中受益，这将推动他们对解决方案的所有权。

• Don’t focus on rare events or the trivial problems—nothing is perfect—but be sure they are not symptoms of systematic machine problems.
• 不要关注罕见事件或琐碎的问题 — 没有什么是完美的 — 但要确保它们不是系统性机器问题的症状。

• Don’t try to find solutions yet. Your focus in this step is strictly on listing the problems.
• 暂时不要试图寻找解决方案。您在此步骤中的重点严格放在列出问题上。

Step 2: Identify the Root Causes. For each problem, identify the deep-seated reason behind the actions that caused each problem. Most problems happen for one of two reasons: 1) It isn’t clear who the Responsible Party is, or 2) The Responsible Party isn’t handling his/her responsibilities well.
第 2 步：确定根本原因。对于每个问题，请确定导致每个问题的操作背后的深层原因。大多数问题的发生有两个原因之一：1） 不清楚谁是责任方，或 2） 责任方没有很好地处理他/她的职责。

You must distinguish proximate causes from root causes. Proximate causes are the reasons or actions that led to the problem. When you start describing the qualities behind these reasons or actions, you are getting closer to the root cause.
您必须区分直接原因和根本原因。“直接原因”是导致问题的原因或操作。当您开始描述这些原因或行为背后的品质时，您就更接近根本原因了。

To get at the root cause, keep asking “Why?” For example:
要找到根本原因，请继续问“为什么？例如：

Problem:
问题：

The team is continually working late and is on the verge of burning out.
团队不断工作到很晚，濒临筋疲力尽。

Why?
为什么？

Because we don’t have enough capacity to meet the demand put on the team.
因为我们没有足够的能力来满足团队的需求。

Why?
为什么？

Because we inherited this new responsibility without additional staff.
因为我们在没有额外员工的情况下继承了这一新责任。

Why?
为什么？

Because the manager did not understand the volume of work before accepting the responsibility.
因为经理在接受责任之前不了解工作量。

Why?
为什么？

Because the manager is bad at anticipating problems and creating plans. [Root Cause]
因为经理不善于预测问题和制定计划。[根本原因]

Do not exclude any relevant people from the drill-down: Besides losing the benefit of their ideas, you’ll disenfranchise them from the game plan and reduce their sense of ownership. At the same time, remember that people tend to be more defensive than self-critical. It is your job as a manager to get at truth and excellence, not to make people happy. For example, the correct path might be to fire some people and replace them with better people, or put them in jobs they might not want. Everyone’s objective must be to get at the best answers, not the answers that will make the most people happy.
不要将任何相关人员排除在深入研究之外：除了失去他们的想法的好处外，您还将剥夺他们参与游戏计划的权利并降低他们的主人翁意识。同时，请记住，人们往往更倾向于防御而不是自我批评。作为管理者，你的工作是追求真理和卓越，而不是让人们快乐。例如，正确的途径可能是解雇一些人，用更好的人取代他们，或者让他们从事他们可能不想要的工作。每个人的目标都必须是最好的答案，而不是让大多数人满意的答案。

You may find that multiple problems identified in Step 1 share the same root cause. Because you are doing a drill-down in a quick session, your root cause diagnoses may only be provisional—essentially alerts about things to watch out for.
您可能会发现步骤 1 中发现的多个问题具有相同的根本原因。由于您正在快速会话中进行深入分析，因此您的根本原因诊断可能只是临时的，本质上是有关需要注意的事项的警报。

When Step 2 is completed, take a break to reflect; then come up with a plan.
完成第 2 步后，请休息一下反思;然后提出一个计划。

Step 3: Create a Plan. Step away from the group and develop a plan that addresses the root causes. Plans are like movie scripts, where you visualize who will do what through time to achieve the goals. They are developed by iterating through multiple possibilities, weighing the likelihood of goal achievement versus costs and risks. They should have specific tasks, outcomes, Responsible Parties, tracking metrics, and timelines. Allow the key people involved to discuss the plan thoroughly. Not everyone needs to agree on the plan but the Responsible Parties and other key people must be in sync.
第 3 步：创建计划。离开小组并制定解决根本原因的计划。计划就像电影剧本，您可以在其中可视化谁将随着时间的推移执行哪些操作来实现目标。它们是通过迭代多种可能性，权衡实现目标的可能性与成本和风险而开发的。他们应该有特定的任务、结果、责任方、跟踪指标和时间表。让参与其中的关键人员彻底讨论该计划。并非每个人都需要就计划达成一致，但责任方和其他关键人员必须保持同步。

Step 4: Execute the Plan. Execute the agreed-upon plan and transparently track its progress. At least monthly, report on the planned and actual progress to date and the expectations for the coming period, and hold people publicly accountable for delivering their outcomes successfully and on time. Make adjustments to the plan as required to reflect reality.
第 4 步：执行计划。执行商定的计划并透明地跟踪其进度。至少每月报告一次迄今为止的计划和实际进展以及对未来一段时间的期望，并让人们对成功和按时交付结果公开负责。根据需要对计划进行调整以反映现实。

12.5 Understand that diagnosis is foundational to both progress and quality relationships.
12.5 了解诊断是进步和质量关系的基础。

If you and others are open-minded and engage in a quality back-and-forth, not only will you find better solutions, you will also get to know each other better. It is an opportunity for you to assess your people and to help them grow—and vice versa.
如果您和其他人思想开放并进行高质量的来回交流，您不仅会找到更好的解决方案，还会更好地了解彼此。这是您评估员工并帮助他们成长的机会，反之亦然。
