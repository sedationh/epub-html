16 And for Heaven’s Sake, Don’t Overlook Governance!
16 看在天堂的份上，不要忽视治理！

All that I’ve said thus far will be useless if you don’t have good governance. Governance is the oversight system that removes the people and the processes if they aren’t working well. It is the process that checks and balances power to assure that the principles and interests of the community as a whole are always placed above the interests and power of any individual or faction. Because power will rule, power must be put in the hands of capable people in key roles who have the right values, do their jobs well, and will check and balance the power of others.
如果你没有良好的治理，我到目前为止所说的 ll 将毫无用处。治理是一种监督系统，如果人员和流程运行不佳，就会将其删除。这是一个制衡权力的过程，以确保整个社区的原则和利益始终凌驾于任何个人或派别的利益和权力之上。因为权力会统治，所以权力必须交到担任关键角色的有能力的人手中，他们有正确的价值观，做好自己的工作，并且会制衡他人的权力。

I didn’t realize the importance of this sort of governance until after I transitioned out of the CEO role, because I was an entrepreneur and company builder (as well as an investment manager) who largely did what I thought was best. While I needed and developed double-checks on myself—I created a Management Committee that I put above me so that I had to report to it—I always had the power of my equity to change things, though I never used it. Some might say that I was a benevolent despot because while I had all the power (the complete voting rights), I exercised my power in an idea-meritocratic way, recognizing that the good of the whole was best for us all, and that I needed to be double-checked. I certainly did not create the sort of governance system appropriate for Bridgewater, given its scale.
直到我从 CEO 角色过渡出来后，我才意识到这种治理的重要性，因为我是一名企业家和公司建设者（也是一名投资经理），在很大程度上做我认为最好的事情。虽然我需要并对自己进行双重检查——我创建了一个管理委员会，我把它放在我之上，这样我就必须向它报告——但我总是有我的资产来改变事情，尽管我从来没有使用它。有些人可能会说我是一个仁慈的暴君，因为虽然我拥有所有的权力（完全的投票权），但我以一种唯贤的理念方式行使我的权力，认识到整体的利益对我们所有人都是最好的，我需要被仔细检查。考虑到布里奇沃特的规模，我当然没有创建适合它的那种治理系统。

For example, Bridgewater didn’t have a board of directors overseeing the CEOs, there were no internal regulations, no judicial system for people to appeal to, and no enforcement system, because we didn’t need them. I, with the help of others, simply created the rules and enforced them, though everyone had the right to appeal and overturn my and others’ judgments. Our principles were the equivalent of what the Articles of Confederation had been to the United States in its first years, and our policies were like our laws, but I never created a formal way of operating such as a “Constitution” or a justice system to enforce them and resolve disputes. As a result, when I stepped out and passed the power to others, confusions about decision rights arose. After conferring with some of the world’s greatest experts on governance, we put a new system in place based on these principles. Still, I want to make clear that I don’t consider myself an expert on governance and can’t vouch for the following principles as much as I can vouch for the previous ones, because they are still new as of this writing.
例如，Bridgewater 没有监督 CEO 的董事会，没有内部法规，没有供人们上诉的司法系统，也没有执法系统，因为我们不需要它们。我在其他人的帮助下，简单地制定了规则并执行它们，尽管每个人都有权上诉并推翻我和其他人的判决。我们的原则相当于《邦联条例》（Articles of Confederation）在美国成立之初之久之于美国，我们的政策就像我们的法律一样，但我从未创造过一种正式的运作方式，如“宪法”或司法系统来执行它们和解决争端。结果，当我走出去，将权力传递给其他人时，出现了关于决策权的困惑。在与一些世界上最伟大的治理专家协商后，我们根据这些原则建立了一个新系统。尽管如此，我想明确表示，我不认为自己是治理方面的专家，并且不能像我能保证以前的原则那样保证以下原则，因为在撰写本文时，它们仍然是新的。

16.1 To be successful, all organizations must have checks and balances.
16.1 要取得成功，所有组织都必须有制衡机制。

By checks, I mean people who check on other people to make sure they’re performing well, and by balances, I mean balances of power. Even the most benevolent leaders are prone to becoming more autocratic, if for no other reason than because managing a lot of people and having limited time to do it requires them to make numerous difficult choices quickly, and they sometimes lose patience with arguments and issue commands instead. And most leaders are not so benevolent that they can be trusted to put the organization’s interests ahead of their own.
我所说的检查，是指检查其他人以确保他们表现良好的人，而平衡，我指的是权力平衡。即使是最仁慈的领导者也容易变得更加专制，如果没有其他原因，只是因为管理很多人而时间有限，这需要他们迅速做出许多艰难的选择，他们有时会对争论失去耐心并发出命令。而且大多数领导者都不是那么仁慈，以至于可以信任他们将组织的利益置于自身利益之上。

a. Even in an idea meritocracy, merit cannot be the only determining factor in assigning responsibility and authority. Appropriate vested interests also need to be taken into consideration. For example, the owners of a company might have vested interests that they are perfectly entitled to that might be at odds with the vested interests of the people in the company who, based on the idea meritocracy, are most believable. That should not lead the owners to simply turn over the keys to those leaders. That conflict has to be worked out. Since the purpose of the idea meritocracy is to produce the best results, and the owners have the rights and powers to assess that, of course they will make the determination—though I recommend they choose wisely.
a. 即使在思想精英制中，功绩也不能成为分配责任和权力的唯一决定因素。还需要考虑适当的既得利益。例如，一家公司的所有者可能拥有他们完全有权享有的既得利益，这可能与公司中基于精英管理理念最可信的人的既得利益相悖。这不应该导致所有者简单地将钥匙交给这些领导者。这种冲突必须得到解决。既然任人唯贤这个想法的目的是产生最好的结果，而所有者有权利和权力来评估这一点，他们当然会做出决定——尽管我建议他们明智地选择。

b. Make sure that no one is more powerful than the system or so important that they are irreplaceable. For an idea meritocracy, it is especially important that its governance system is more powerful than any individual—and that it directs and constrains its leaders rather than the other way around. The Chinese leader Wang Qishan drew my attention to what happened in ancient Rome when Julius Caesar revolted against the government, defeated his fellow general Pompey, seized control of the Republic from the Senate, and named himself emperor for life. Even after he was assassinated and governance by the Senate was restored, Rome would never again be what it was; the era of civil strife that followed was more damaging than any foreign war.
b. 确保没有人比系统更强大，或者重要到他们不可替代。对于一个思想精英制来说，其治理体系比任何个人都更强大，并且它指导和约束其领导者，而不是相反，这一点尤为重要。中国领导人王岐山提请我注意古罗马发生的事情，当时尤利乌斯·凯撒 （Julius Caesar） 起义反抗政府，击败了他的同僚庞培将军，从元老院夺取了共和国的控制权，并自封为终身皇帝。即使在他被暗杀并恢复元老院的统治之后，罗马也永远不会再是原来的样子;随之而来的内乱时代比任何对外战争都更具破坏性。

c. Beware of fiefdoms. While it’s great for teams and departments to feel a strong bond of shared purpose, loyalty to a boss or department head cannot be allowed to conflict with loyalty to the organization as a whole. Fiefdoms are counterproductive and contrary to the values of an idea meritocracy.
c. 当心封地。虽然团队和部门感受到共同目标的牢固纽带是件好事，但不能允许对老板或部门主管的忠诚与对整个组织的忠诚发生冲突。封地会适得其反，与思想精英统治的价值观背道而驰。

d. Make clear that the organization’s structure and rules are designed to ensure that its checks-and-balances system functions well. Every organization has its own way of doing this. The diagram on the next page is a sketch of my conceptualization of how this should work for Bridgewater, which is currently an organization of about 1,500 people. The principles it follows, however, are universal; I believe that all organizations need some version of this basic structure.
d. 明确组织的结构和规则旨在确保其制衡系统运作良好。每个组织都有自己的方法。下一页的图表是我对 Bridgewater 应该如何运作的概念化草图，该组织目前是一个约有 1,500 人的组织。然而，它遵循的原则是普遍的;我相信所有组织都需要这种基本结构的某种版本。

There are one to three chairmen working with seven to fifteen board members supported by staff, whose purpose is primarily to assess whether: 1) The people running the company are capable; 2) The company is operating in accordance with its agreed-upon principles and rules. The board has the power to select and replace the CEOs, but doesn’t engage in the micromanagement of the firm nor the people running it, though in the event of an emergency, they can drop into a more active role. (They can also help the CEOs to the extent they want it.) While Bridgewater’s idea meritocracy is ideally all-inclusive, there need to be various circles of authority, trust and access to information, and decision-making authority, which are shown in the chart’s three circles.
有 1 到 3 名董事长与 7 到 15 名董事会成员一起工作，并有员工支持，其主要目的是评估：1） 经营公司的人是否有能力;2） 公司按照其商定的原则和规则运营。董事会有权选择和更换首席执行官，但不参与公司的微观管理，也不参与公司的管理人员，但在紧急情况下，他们可以发挥更积极的作用。（他们也可以在 CEO 们需要的范围内提供帮助。虽然布里奇沃特的思想精英管理在理想情况下是包罗万象的，但需要有各种权威圈子、信任和信息获取以及决策权，如图表的三个圆圈所示。

e. Make sure reporting lines are clear. While this is important throughout the organization, it is especially important that the reporting lines of the board (those doing the oversight) are independent of the reporting lines of the CEOs (those doing the management), though there should be cooperation between them.
e. 确保报告行清晰。虽然这在整个组织中都很重要，但董事会的汇报线（负责监督的人）独立于首席执行官（负责管理的人）的汇报线尤为重要，尽管他们之间应该有合作。

f. Make sure decision rights are clear. Make sure it’s clear how much weight each person’s vote has so that if a decision must be made when there is still disagreement, there is no doubt how to resolve it.
f. 确保决策权明确。确保清楚每个人的投票有多大权重，这样如果在仍然存在分歧时必须做出决定，毫无疑问如何解决它。

g. Make sure that the people doing the assessing 1) have the time to be fully informed about how the person they are checking on is doing, 2) have the ability to make the assessments, and 3) are not in a conflict of interest that stands in the way of carrying out oversight effectively. In order to assess well, one has to gain a threshold level of understanding and that takes time. Some people have the ability and the courage to hold people accountable, while most don’t; having such ability and courage is essential. And the person doing the assessing must not have conflicts of interest—such as being in a subordinate position to the person they are intended to check on—that stand in the way of holding them accountable, including recommending that they be fired.
g. 确保进行评估的人员 1） 有时间充分了解他们正在检查的人的情况，2） 有能力进行评估，以及 3） 没有妨碍有效进行监督的利益冲突。为了评估得好，一个人必须获得一个阈值的理解水平，这需要时间。有些人有能力和勇气让人们承担责任，而大多数人则没有;拥有这样的能力和勇气是必不可少的。进行评估的人不得有利益冲突，例如处于他们打算检查的人的从属地位，这些利益冲突会妨碍追究他们的责任，包括建议解雇他们。

h. Recognize that decision makers must have access to the information necessary to make decisions and must be trustworthy enough to handle that information safely. That doesn’t mean that all people must have access and be trustworthy. It is possible to have subcommittees who have access to sensitive information and make recommendations to the board that are substantiated with enough information to make good judgments, but without disclosing the highly sensitive particulars.
h. 认识到决策者必须能够访问做出决策所需的信息，并且必须足够值得信赖才能安全地处理这些信息。这并不意味着所有人都必须有访问权限并且值得信赖。可以让小组委员会获得敏感信息并向董事会提出建议，这些小组委员会有足够的信息来做出正确的判断，但不会披露高度敏感的细节。

16.2 Remember that in an idea meritocracy a single CEO is not as good as a great group of leaders.
16.2 请记住，在理念任人唯贤的情况下，单个 CEO 不如一群伟大的领导者好。

Dependence on one person produces too much key-man risk, limits the range of expertise (because nobody is good at everything), and fails to establish adequate checks and balances. It also creates a burden because there’s generally too much to do. That’s why we have a co-CEO model at Bridgewater that is essentially a partnership of two or three people who lead the firm.
对一个人的依赖会产生太多的关键人物风险，限制了专业知识的范围（因为没有人什么都擅长），并且无法建立足够的制衡。它还会产生负担，因为通常有太多事情要做。这就是为什么我们在 Bridgewater 采用联合 CEO 模式，本质上是由两到三个领导公司的人合伙。

At Bridgewater the CEOs are overseen by a board largely via the executive chairman or chairmen. In our idea meritocracy, the CEOs are also held accountable by the employees of the company, even though these employees are subordinate to the CEOs. The challenge of having two or three people is for them to dance well together. If they can’t do that, and coordinate well with the chairmen, they have to notify the executive chairman or chairmen so changes can be made.
在 Bridgewater，首席执行官主要通过执行董事长或董事长董事会进行监督。在我们的理念任人唯贤中，首席执行官也由公司员工负责，即使这些员工从属于首席执行官。有两三个人的挑战是让他们一起跳舞。如果他们不能做到这一点，并且不能与主席进行良好的协调，他们必须通知执行主席或主席，以便做出改变。

For the same reason we have more than one CEO overseeing management of the company, we have more than one chief investment officer (there are currently three).
出于同样的原因，我们有不止一位 CEO 监督公司的管理，我们有不止一位首席投资官（目前有三位）。

16.3 No governance system of principles, rules, and checks and balances can substitute for a great partnership.
16.3 任何由原则、规则和制衡组成的治理体系都无法取代良好的伙伴关系。

All these principles, rules, and checks and balances won’t be worth much if you don’t have capable people in positions of power who instinctually want to operate for the good of the community based on the agreed-upon principles. A company’s leaders must have wisdom, competence, and the ability to have close, cooperative, and effective working relationships characterized by both thoughtful disagreement and commitment to following through with whatever the idea-meritocratic process decides.
如果你没有有能力的人担任权力职位，他们本能地想根据商定的原则为社区的利益运作，那么所有这些原则、规则和制衡都不会有太大价值。公司的领导者必须具有智慧、能力和能力，能够建立密切、合作和有效的工作关系，其特点是深思熟虑的分歧和致力于贯彻执行理念-精英过程决定的任何决定。

We work with others to get three things:
我们与他人合作以获得三件事：

1) Leverage to accomplish our chosen missions in bigger and better ways than we could alone.
1） 利用杠杆以比我们单独行动的更大、更好的方式完成我们选择的使命。

2) Quality relationships that together make for a great community.
2） 高质量的关系共同构成一个伟大的社区。

3) Money that allows us to buy what we need and want for ourselves and others.
3） 让我们为自己和他人购买我们需要和想要的东西的钱。
